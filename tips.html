Tips & Guides

Not over-complicated theory. Just real-life basics that keep you safer and make trips smoother.

Basic Packing List for Bike Trips

  • Riding gear: helmet, gloves, jacket, knee guards.
  • Rain gear: raincoat, waterproof covers for bags.
  • Tools: puncture kit, basic spanner set, torch.
  • Essentials: water, energy bars, power bank, documents.

Riding in the Mountains

  • Ride slow in unknown curves, never cut blind corners.
  • Use engine braking; don’t hold the brakes constantly downhill.
  • Watch out for loose gravel, animals, and trucks.
  • Start early in the day, avoid riding late nights in hills.

Altitude & Health

  • Climb gradually, don’t rush long distances on Day 1.
  • Hydrate well and avoid heavy meals while riding.
  • Know basic signs of altitude sickness: headache, nausea, dizziness – and when to rest or go down.

Group Ride Etiquette

  • Maintain formation, don’t overtake aggressively inside group.
  • Wait at turns/junctions so no one gets lost.
  • Agree on signals for fuel, food, breaks, and emergencies.
